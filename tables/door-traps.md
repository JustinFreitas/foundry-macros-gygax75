# Door Traps (d20)

A table to roll on when a dungeon door turns up **trapped** (after the usual
stuck / locked / trapped check). Nothing exotic — classic dungeon hazards. Each
result lists the effect and at least one way a careful party can **circumvent**
it.

**Before you open any suspect door:** listen at it, **search** for traps (thief
or elf chance, or the DM's call), and where possible open it from the **side**
with a **10-ft pole, rope, or hook** rather than by hand. **Find/Remove Traps**
disarms a trap once it's been found. Effects below assume OSE/B-X-style saves and
damage; adjust dice to taste.

Roll **1d20**:

| d20 | Trap | Effect | Circumvent |
|:---:|------|--------|------------|
| 1  | **Poisoned needle** | A needle in the lock or handle pricks the opener — save vs. poison or suffer its effect (DM's poison: damage, sleep, or weakness). | Spike/jam the lock; open with tools or a gloved hand, never bare skin; Remove Traps. |
| 2  | **Falling block** | A stone block drops in the doorway as it opens — save vs. paralysis (or Dexterity) or take 2d6 damage. | Search to spot the seam; brace/wedge the block; trigger it from the side with a pole. |
| 3  | **Alarm (gong or bell)** | Opening rings a hidden bell or gong — make a wandering-monster check, and anything nearby is alerted. | Spot and muffle the wire/clapper; oil the hinges; pick the lock quietly and ease the door. |
| 4  | **Pit beneath the threshold** | The floor in the doorway drops as the door swings — fall 10 ft (1d6) or more. | Tap the floor ahead with a pole; open the door and step back; lay a plank across. |
| 5  | **Gas jet (sleep or poison)** | A jet of gas vents from the frame — save vs. poison or fall asleep / be sickened. | Hold your breath and back away; detect and plug the nozzle; pull the door open from a distance with a rope. |
| 6  | **Dart trap** | Darts fire from ports in the frame — 1d4 each, a save halves the number that hit. | Search for and jam the ports; stand to the side while opening; pull the door with a hook. |
| 7  | **Scything blade** | A blade sweeps across the doorway — 1d8 damage to anyone in the gap. | Spot the slot it rides in; trip it with a pole first, then pass; or time the swing. |
| 8  | **Shock handle** | The handle is charged — touching it deals 1d6 and a save or you drop what you're holding. | Wear an insulated glove; open with a pole or rope; douse/ground the handle first. |
| 9  | **Flame jet** | A gout of fire erupts on opening — 2d6 damage, save for half. | Open from the side with a rope; detect and block the nozzle; soak yourself/your gear first. |
| 10 | **Spring-loaded crossbow** | A concealed crossbow fires a bolt at whoever opens the door — 1d8 damage. | Open from the side, out of the line of fire; find the low trip-wire and trip it with a pole. |
| 11 | **Delayed ceiling collapse** | A grinding rumble, then the ceiling at the threshold caves in after 1 round — heavy damage to anyone beneath. | Heed the warning sound and clear the doorway at once; brace the frame; don't linger to loot. |
| 12 | **Magical glyph / rune** | A glowing sigil on the door discharges when touched (fear, shock, or damage per the DM). | Read Magic to identify it; scratch out or cover the rune; Dispel Magic; open without touching the warded panel. |
| 13 | **Portcullis drop** | An iron grate slams down — behind the lead character, splitting the party. | Spot the slot in the ceiling above; jam it with a spear or spike; move the whole party through quickly together. |
| 14 | **Acid spray** | A corrosive jet sprays the opener — damage plus a piece of armor or gear is ruined. | Open from a distance with a rope; raise a shield; detect and plug the nozzle. |
| 15 | **False "stuck" door** | The "stuck" door is solid and immovable; the real catch is elsewhere. Time and effort wasted force a wandering-monster check. | Search for the true catch or a hidden door nearby; don't waste rounds brute-forcing it. |
| 16 | **Glue / seizing lock** | An adhesive or seizing mechanism binds the opener's hand or tools fast to the handle. | Use a tool, not a bare hand; apply solvent or oil; cut yourself free (and lose the tool). |
| 17 | **Spiked threshold** | Hidden spikes thrust up from the floor as you step through — damage to feet/legs. | Tap the floor first with a pole; step well over the threshold; heavy boots or a board help. |
| 18 | **Net / snare** | A weighted net or snare drops on the opener, entangling them for a round or two. | Spot the seam or trigger overhead; set it off from the side; cut free quickly. |
| 19 | **Symbol of summoning** | Opening the door rings or flares a sigil that summons a guardian creature. | Detect and disable the sigil; Dispel Magic; or simply be ready for a fight before you open it. |
| 20 | **Teleport / one-way door** | Crossing the threshold shunts the opener elsewhere — another room, a cell, or back the way they came. | Detect the dweomer first; rope the party together so one person can be pulled back; scout it with Detect Magic before stepping through. |
